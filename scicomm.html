Who am I?

A Brief History of Myself

Hello! My name is Kamran (or Kammy) Bogue and I'm currently a PhD researcher at the University of Manchester, specifically at the Jodrell Bank Centre for Astrophysics, or 'JBCA'.

I was born and raised in West Molesey [south west London], and even before I went to University I was lucky enough to -> Took part in the Social Mobility Foundation's "Aspiring Professionals Programme". Did the 'Insights work experience'. I was already interested in teaching and outreach at this stage, and began tutoring while I was sixth form student.

I began studying my MPhys Physics with Astronomy undergraduate at Cardiff University in 2016. Excited as ever by science outreach, I started a part time rule at Techniquest Science Discovery Centre as a Science Communicator.

Volunteered with Universe in The Classroom as a Stellar Role Model. Help organise and run astronomy workshops for primary school children.

I was a Student Mentor, as well as a Mentor Consultant.

Social Secretary for the Cardiff University Astronomy Soceity, ran trips to the Brecon Beacons with telescopes. Wrote for Gair Rhydd, the Cardiff University Student Newspaper.

It was also while I was still an undergraduate at Cardiff University that I was able to partake in a summer placement at the International Centre for Radio Astronomy Research ('ICRAR') with Aaron Robotham and Claudia Lagos Urbina. Beforehand learned R - new progamming language - and was there for 3 months. Confirmed that a PhD was the right next step for me.

I graduated with a first from my MPhys.
